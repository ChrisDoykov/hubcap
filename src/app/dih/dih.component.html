Digital Innovation Hubs


Our network of Digital Innovation Hubs (DIHs) gives SMEs an easy entry route to Model-Based Design (MBD) for Cyber-Physical Systems (CPSs). The network offers a comprehensive set of digital innovation services, including supporting businesses to experiment, find investments and business opportunities, and access skills and training.




Austria

HUBCAP Austria was hosted by the Virtual Vehicle Research Center GmbH which is Europe’s largest R&D center for virtual vehicle technology with 300 employees. Their research priority is the linking of numerical simulations and hardware testing, which leads to a powerful HW-SW whole system design and automation of testing and validation procedures. Following this focus on industry-related research VIRTUAL VEHICLE is the innovation catalyst for future vehicle technologies.

The international partner network of VIRTUAL VEHICLE consists of around 100 national and international industrial partners (OEMs, Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers as well as software providers) as well as over 40 national and international scientific institutions.

Denmark

HUBCAP Denmark specialises in software engineering for complex Cyber-Physical Systems, including system modelling methodologies and tools (Model-Based Design, Vienna Development Method), predictive and software analysis (formal methods, theorem proving, and machine learning), and co-simulation. These core areas of expertise are seamlessly integrated within Digital Twin platforms. Aarhus University’s Digital Innovation Hub provided expertise to its collaborators in the areas covered by its two main centres:

  • The Centre for Digitalisation, Big Data and Data Analytics ( “DIGIT”)
  • The AU Centre for Digital Twins.

Germany

HUBCAP Germany was hosted by fortiss in Munich. Fortiss provided expertise in a range of fields including model-based software and systems engineering, model-based synthesis, design space exploration, analysis and design of dependable systems, and software engineering of smart CPS in various domains such as automotive, smart energy, or industrial automation. A particular emphasis was laid on the intersection of software engineering and the increasingly important field of Artificial Intelligence. To this end, HUBCAP Germany built on the Munich Innovation Hub for Applied AI, which integrates AI research with innovation support activities for entrepreneurs, startups, and SMEs, to foster and accelerate the adoption of AI solutions by industry.

Sweden

In Sweden HUBCAP was represented by the KTH Digital Innovation Hub on Industrial Digitalization, the DIHID. Located at the Mechatronics Division at KTH in Stockholm, the DIHID acted as a meeting ground for Tech Scaleups, Industry and Academic Faculties. They offered expertise, testbeds and professional training within the domains of Robotics, Model Based Design, Autonomous Systems and Machine Learning. DIHID also organized expert talks and conferences to share knowledge between engineering/scientific disciplines and academia-industry. Furthermore, DIHID was involved in the PECA initiative, which offered a new approach on life-long learning for Swedish Industry.

Their clients work with industrial digitalization on both a strategic and technical level. They are leaders and visionaries, practitioners and strategists who develop and produce products, services and knowledge. We had a close partnership, via our industry network ICES, with more than 30 companies and organizations.

Italy

HUBCAP Italy was led by Politecnico di Milano, located in Milan, while a satellite centre was hosted by Fondazione Bruno Kessler, located in Trento. The Milan site focused on the manufacturing industry, hosting an Industry 4.0 Lab experimental facility, a CPS-based training and experimental facility. The centre plays a leading role in the Milan Competence Center (MADE in Italy) of the Industry 4.0 Italian National Plan, carrying out experiments in Industry 4.0 including CPS manufacturing. The Trento satellite focuses on high quality research with impact to market and society, including expertise and tools for MBD, verification, validation, and safety analysis. Besides the physical spaces, both sites offered services such as training courses, support for participating in research projects, sharing of utilities costs and IT services.

Romania

HUBCAP Romania was hosted by Lucian Blaga University of Sibiu and was led by the Connected Intelligence Research Center (INCON) complemented by Hasso Plattner Institute for Knowledge Transfer (HPI). INCON focuses on theoretical and application-driven research for modelling, simulation and deployment of cyber physical social systems. It provides a CPS-based experimental facility comprising of: a standard Festo MPS500 Didactic System, a custom OTG-Usb stick assembly demonstrator (Small Scale I4.0 Production system), a custom production system for assembling smart products (Large Scale I4.0 Production System) and finally a training station with Augmented and Virtual Reality for manual operations to assemble smart products considering the operator’s state (I4.0 Training station). HPI is a ULBS foundation established in 2018 focusing on linking regional companies and clusters (e.g. Smart City Sibiu, Cluj IT, PrelMet Transilvania, etc.) to support RDI and training in the field of digitisation. It provides RDI and training services to all interested organisations based on the INCON infrastructure.

Lucian Blaga University of Sibiu, Faculty of Engineering, Emil Cioran 4, Sibiu 550025, Romania bogdan.pirvu@ulbsibiu.ro centers.ulbsibiu.ro

United Kindgom

HUBCAP UK, was hosted at Newcastle University, offered expertise in MBD for embedded systems and Systems of Systems, and a shared programme of research, engagement, innovation and technology transfer on digitally enabled urban systems. This cluster contains labs on CPS, the National Innovation Centre for Data, and national centres for research and innovation in green infrastructure and energy systems integration, an Urban Observatory, and a “Decision Theatre” exploring human decision-making through interaction with large and complex data. HUBCAP UK aimed to lower barriers to innovation in CPS and CPS-enabled products and services. Its two main focuses were:

  • Development of application domains in digitally enabled sustainable urban systems.
  • Support for model-based CPS design by collaborative modelling and co-simulation, and transfer of well-founded techniques in the engineering of dependable systems into CPS engineering.

Services

HUBCAP partners offer a variety of services described below. Please contact the Service Centre who will direct your query:

Service Centre: info@hubcap.eu

Fast Prototyping and Validation

Our platform allows CPS solutions to be prototyped and validated using cloud technologies in a “sandboxed” environment, which requires no installation for the user, and where models selected from catalogues can be combined with legacy models from the end-user. Sandboxed services will be implemented in our HUBCAP collaboration platform and will provide functionalities such as CPS multi-modelling, co-simulation, monitoring and analytics, composition and orchestration, enhanced with protection mechanisms to ensure the security and privacy of the data exchanged within the collaboration platform.

Access to Market and Funding

Your SME get help to access funding opportunities for your development projects. We will share information about different funding opportunities involving companies, funding agencies and investors, or potential research and innovation projects. Events are organised to share information about possible public or private funding opportunities, available from both within the DIH region and nationally/internationally, e.g. in the HUBCAP DIH network.

Knowledge Services

From awareness-raising to training and skills development for model-based CPS engineering. We will be offering information events, seminars, and training courses on the HUBCAP technologies and tools.

Digital Twin Accelerators

The Digital Twin Accelerator is a new service that helps you access the expertise of four international Digital Innovation Hubs and their networks of hundreds of specialists to demystify Digital Twin technology. We can help you learn the basics of Digital Twins through short briefings for business leaders; explore the opportunities for your business to develop the knowledge and skills you need to understand the opportunities and barriers to exploiting Digital Twin technology in your sector.

Technology Services

These enable hands-on experience on HUBCAP technologies, provided through support for SMEs engaged in Open Call (OC) experiments and by organising practical labs such as hackathons for the rapid prototyping of use cases. We can help match your SME with the right expertise and tools for model-based design, verification, validation, and safety analysis.

Ecosystem Building

These services will help you reach out to stakeholders, businesses, government and expertise in the regions round our Digital Hubs, to help needs and demands with offers and opportunities. We will do this through community events for stakeholders from industry, research, or finance, and ideation events, e.g. based on design thinking, to inform and implement the services provided by HUBCAP, and encouraging follow-up collaboration of the participants.

External Evaluators for HUBCAP Open Call #3 INNOVATE

HUBCAP aims to set up an External Pool of Evaluators (EPE) to support the review process of the proposals received from the HUBCAP Call #3 INNOVATE open call. The EPE gathers a set of recognized experts in Model-Based Design (MBD) Cyber-Physical Systems (CPSs) domains selected through an open process to ensure transparent and consistent evaluation of the proposals.

Evaluators profile

We are looking for experts in the Model-Based Design (MBD) tools and Cyber-Physical Systems (CPSs) domains with experience in evaluating EU funded projects and a proven track record in the technical areas corresponding to HUBCAP Call #3 INNOVATE open call.

Need more information regarding the open call? Please visit the open call dedicated page.

What are the benefits for the evaluators?

  1. Get to know innovative solutions in the MBD CPS domains
  2. Play a role in the selection of SMEs
  3. Be reimbursed for evaluation work
  4. Connect with SMEs
  5. Be an important part of the HUBCAP project and related ecosystem

Services required

Services will be carried out remotely and will involve evaluation of the applications received, and participation in consensus meetings. All evaluators will be required to sign a non-conflict of interest declaration and a contract with the HUBCAP project coordinator, before being accepted to evaluate a specific application.

Remuneration

Evaluators will be reimbursed for their time and effort on the basis of the number of proposals evaluated, with each proposal corresponding to a value of €75, considering 1 hour to evaluate each proposal.

Need more information? Feel free to get in touch: info@hubcap.eu and we will get back to you shortly!

Important Notice

Please note that this expression of interest to participate in HUBCAP Call #3 INNOVATE evaluation as an external expert is NOT binding and does NOT constitute any commitment to the HUBCAP project.

The selection of the experts to support HUBCAP in the evaluation of the HUBCAP Call #3 INNOVATE will be taken at a later stage, since it depends on the number of received proposals, their origin, the domain that they target as well as the origin of the potential evaluators, their affiliations, their expertise, and the balance between various criteria (e.g., gender, background, affiliation type, expertise etc.). Furthermore, we are obliged to frequently refresh the pool of evaluators that provides support on the proposal’s evaluation.

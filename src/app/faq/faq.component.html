About the HUBCAP Programme

Q: What are CPS?

A: Cyber-Physical Systems are systems that link the physical world (e.g., through sensors or actuators) with the digital world of software systems and communications technology. A CPS will often include components from many different providers, so your business may already be contributing towards CPSs.

There is growing demand for autonomous, secure CPSs that can interact with humans while respecting privacy. This creates opportunities for SMEs in an ever-wider range of domains, but in order to take them, businesses must leverage advances in engineering technology across sectors and regions. Model-Based Design (MBD) tools help you represent and analyse products digitally before realising them in hardware and software, by building digital twins and using simulation for collaborative diagnostics and decision making.

Q: What is Model-Based Design (MBD) ?

A: Model-Based Design (MBD) is a method to address the design of complex systems with models. It prescribes the use of models throughout the development process in order to represent system structure and behaviors, providing a basis for machine-assisted analysis of system properties, and supporting design decisions through processes of refinement into implementation. The purposes it serves are:

  • 1) to reduce the complexity of design by abstraction;
  • 2) to ensure the quality of the system by rigorous analysis of its properties;
  • 3) to reduce the cost of the development by detecting issues in the early phases of the development.

Some examples of popular techniques used in MBD include model checking, simulation, model-based safety analysis, etc. If your SME has utilized any of these during the development process of your asset or if you make software tools that allow others to use these techniques then you are indeed working with Model-Based Design and are eligible to apply for one of our PULL calls.

MBD is quite standard in software engineering and is becoming more and more relevant in system engineering, where it must integrate methods for control engineering and safety engineering.

Q: What will HUBCAP collaboration platform offer?

A: The HUBCAP platform (based on DIHIWARE) will offer a complete collaboration environment, inspired by Enterprise Social Software principles, supporting both “Access to” and “Collaborate with” services. It will provide companies with access to the latest knowledge, expertise, and technology during their Digital Transformation paths toward piloting, testing, and experimenting with new Model-Based Design technologies for Cyber-Physical Systems. The HUBCAP platform includes a sandbox function where MBD tools and models are accessible in the cloud, allowing companies to “test before they invest” in these digital technologies. Q: What will an applicant need in order to connect it’s asset application with HUBCAP platform?

A: The HUBCAP platform works as follows: Software Tools (Applications) in the Platform are executed in a Sandbox. A Sandbox is a customized Virtual Machine, running an OS (a minimal installation of Linux or Windows) and containing a Tool. As a Tool Provider, you will need to customise the Virtual Machine and OS to run your Tool. This read-to-use Virtual Machine will then be saved into the Tools Catalogue. Users can then access the Platform using a web browser and select one or more Tools from the Tool Catalogue to load into the Sandbox.

So, integration primarily involves setting up a VM with any installation scripts and dependencies needed to execute your tool.

Q: I discovered an error in my application/proposal – can I submit it again?

A: Yes. If you discover an error in your proposal you can at any time – provided the call deadline has not expired – submit a new version of your proposal in F6S platform for the HUBCAP programme. Only the last version received before the call deadline will be considered in the evaluation. For this purpose, you are required to send an email to support@f6s.com requesting to reopen your application. Since this procedure takes some time, we recommend making it with time and not in the last days before the deadline.

Q: I believe there was an error in the evaluation of my application. Can I request for a second evaluation?

A: Yes, it is possible to request a second evaluation. Within 3 working days of the delivery of a rejection letter considering the asset application as non-eligible or an ESR, a proposer may submit a request for redress if s/he believes the results of the eligibility checks have not been correctly applied, or if s/he feels that there has been a shortcoming in the way his/her asset application has been evaluated that may affect the final decision on whether to enter the Call #1 PULL or not. Requests must be

  • Related to the evaluation process or eligibility checks.
  • Clearly describe the complaint.
  • Received within the time limit (3 working days) from the reception of a rejection letter considering the asset application as non-eligible or the ESR information letter delivered
  • Sent by the SME legal representative that has also submitted the asset application.

The committee will review the complaint and will recommend an appropriate course of action. If there is clear evidence of a shortcoming that could affect the eventual funding decision, it is possible that all or part of the asset application will be re-evaluated.

Please note:

  • This procedure is concerned only with the evaluation and/or eligibility checking process. The committee will not call into question the scientific or technical judgement of appropriately qualified experts.
  • A re-evaluation will only be carried out if there is evidence of a shortcoming that affects the final decision on whether to fund it or not. This means, for example, that a problem relating to one evaluation criterion will not lead to a re-evaluation if an asset application has failed anyway on other criteria.
  • RThe evaluation score following any re-evaluation will be regarded as definitive. It may be lower than the original score.

Only one request for redress per asset application will be considered by the committee. All requests for redress will be treated in confidence and must be sent to Project Coordinator via the F6S platform

Q: Who will decide if an asset application will be selected or not for Call #1 PULL?

A: All proposals submitted on time will be evaluated throughout an evaluation procedure described in the Guide for Applicants. The evaluation will be held by an internal evaluation committee composed by experts from DIHs, partners of HUBCAP consortium.

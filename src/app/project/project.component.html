Project Details

HUBCAP is an Innovation Action supported by the European Union's Horizon 2020 research and innovation programme (contract No 872698) and by the Commissioned Research of National Institute of Information. The action is delivered by a consortium of SMEs, innovation organisations and research institutions committed to developing a sustainable network of businesses, Digital Innovation Hubs (DIHs) and other actors to encourage suppliers and users of Cyber-Physical Systems (CPSs) models and Model- Based Design (MBD) tools to meet and collaborate.

Project Partners

HUBCAP brings together 17 partners across 10 countries, each playing a vital role in delivering the HUBCAP vision.

Digital Innovation Hubs

Our seven DIHs are supported by eight partners and provide access to expertise and training for SMEs to get started with the ecosystem and its technologies. The DIHs are led by:

  • Aarhus University (AU), Denmark
  • Newcastle University (UNEW), UK
  • Fortiss GmbH (FOR), Germany
  • Virtual Vehicle Research Center (VV), Austria
  • Fondazione Bruno Kessler (FBK), Italy
  • KTH Royal Institute of Technology (KTH), Sweden
  • University “Lucian Blaga” of Sibiu (ULBS), Romania
  • Politecnico di Milano (POLIMI), Italy

Open Calls

Our open calls will support SMEs around Europe to join the HUBCAP ecosystem and experiment with MBD technologies for CPS. Our calls are managed by:

  • F6S Network Limited (F6S), Ireland

Seed SME

Our six seed SMEs act as the leaders in creating our innovation ecosystem:

  • Unparallel Innovation (UNP), Portugal
  • Controllab Products (CLP), Netherlands
  • BEIA Consult (BEIA), Romania
  • Verified Systems International (VSI), Germany
  • Validas (VAL), Germany
  • Technology Transfer Systems srl (TTS), Italy

Model-Based Design


The MBD expertise in HUBCAP includes experiences from the Modelica foundation and its support of the Functional Mock-up Interface (FMI) standard. This area of our business is led by:

  • Research Institutes of Sweden AB (RISE), Sweden

Collaborative Platform

Our collaborative platform will give access to MBD technologies and act as a gateway for new and existing members of the ecosystem. It is led by:

  • Engineering Ingegneria Informatica S.p.A. (ENGIT), Italy
